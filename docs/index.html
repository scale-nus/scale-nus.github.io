About Us

SCALE — Safe Control and Learning Group
Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering
National University of Singapore

How can machines learn from data and still remain provably safe when the world is uncertain? That is the question that inspires our work. At SCALE, we develop the theory and algorithms for safe learning-based control, from Bayesian models of unknown dynamics and event-triggered adaptation to generative models for embodied agents and real-time safety filters.

We bring these ideas to life on robotic systems, with a single vision: autonomy you can trust. Our goal is systems that improve with experience, reason about risk, and keep safety at the core of every decision.

Research Areas

Four interconnected themes

Event-triggered Learning Control

Learn and update controllers only when needed — cutting communication and compute while preserving closed-loop guarantees.

Bayesian Learning

Probabilistic models and uncertainty quantification for data-driven control under limited and noisy data.

Generative Models for Embodied Systems

Generative AI for perception, planning, and control in robots — with structure and constraints for real embodiment.

Safe Learning Control

Safety filters, control barrier functions, and risk-aware methods with formal guarantees for real-time autonomy.
