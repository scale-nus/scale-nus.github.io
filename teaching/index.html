Teaching

Courses in control, learning, and embodied AI

SCLABLE members teach and contribute to courses that connect classical control, modern optimization, and learning for autonomous systems.

16-886: Embodied AI

Offered: Spring 2026, Spring 2025

Embodied AI studies how intelligent agents perceive, reason, and act in the physical world. The course covers foundations and recent advances in robot learning, world models, and the integration of generative methods with control and safety constraints for embodied systems.

16-867: Linear Systems

Offered: Fall 2025, Fall 2026

A rigorous introduction to linear dynamical systems, including state-space modeling, controllability and observability, stability, and modern linear control design methods that underpin advanced topics in optimal control and learning-based control.

ROAS 6000I: Optimal Control Systems

Optimal control is an effective approach that has been widely used in robotics and autonomous systems. This course aims to provide students with a firm foundation in optimality principles in modern control system design. Fundamental key concepts in optimal control are introduced, including Hamiltonian, Pontryagin’s minimum principle, Bellman equation, dynamic programming, etc., which bring the prospect of formal linkage to reinforcement learning techniques. Different optimal control methods are also covered in this course, such as LQR, Kalman filter, LQG, MPC, etc. Additionally, students will have the opportunity to discover and learn cutting-edge methodologies in the related field and develop expertise in optimal control system design.
