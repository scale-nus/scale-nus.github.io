Research Highlights

Safe and Control Learning Lab · NUS ECE

We build algorithms that learn from data, reason about uncertainty, and enforce safety on robots and other embodied systems. Below, each theme lists representative works with a figure and short summary. For the complete list, see Publications.

Event-triggered Learning Control

Update models and controllers only when needed — cutting communication and compute while preserving closed-loop guarantees for networked and resource-constrained robots.

Safe Barrier-Constrained Control of Uncertain Systems via Event-triggered Learning

Armin Lederer, A. Begzadic, S. Hirche, J. Cortes, and S. Herbert · IEEE Transactions on Automatic Control, 2026

Continuous high-rate learning is often unnecessary for maintaining safety certificates. This work couples control barrier functions with event-triggered learning so that models and certificates are updated only when uncertainty or performance criteria require it — reducing resource use while keeping barrier constraints valid under model uncertainty.

PDF Preprint Details

Safe Event-Triggered Learning for Sampled-Data Systems

A. Begzadic, Armin Lederer, S. Hirche, S. Herbert, and J. Cortes · American Control Conference (ACC), 2026

We develop event-triggered learning for sampled-data control, triggering model updates from safety and performance monitors rather than a fixed clock. The approach targets digital implementations where sensing, learning, and actuation run at different rates, with formal conditions that preserve safety between trigger times.

Networked Online Learning for Control of Safety-Critical Resource-Constrained Systems

Armin Lederer, M. Zhang, S. Tesfazgi, and S. Hirche · IEEE CCTA, 2022

When robots have limited onboard compute, learning can be offloaded to the cloud — but communication and delay matter. This work studies networked online Gaussian-process learning for safety-critical control under resource constraints, balancing local reactivity with remote model updates.

Bayesian Learning

Probabilistic models and uncertainty quantification for data-driven control — turning limited, noisy data into guarantees that controllers can use in real time.

Uniform Error Bounds for Gaussian Process Regression with Application to Safe Control

Armin Lederer, J. Umlauft, and S. Hirche · NeurIPS, 2019

Standard GP confidence intervals are pointwise; safe control needs bounds that hold uniformly over a region. We derive uniform error bounds for GP regression and show how they enable safety-critical control synthesis under unknown dynamics.

Gaussian Process Uniform Error Bounds with Unknown Hyperparameters for Safety-Critical Applications

A. Capone, Armin Lederer, and S. Hirche · ICML, 2022

Hyperparameters are rarely known exactly, yet many GP safety certificates assume they are fixed. This work provides uniform error bounds that account for unknown hyperparameters, closing a gap between theory and practical Bayesian learning for safety-critical systems.

Policy Search via Bayesian Optimization with Temporal Difference Gaussian Processes

Armin Lederer, A. Srivastava, M. Bagatella, and A. Krause · ICML, 2026

We combine Bayesian optimization with temporal-difference Gaussian processes for sample-efficient policy search. The approach exploits temporal structure in value estimation so that scarce interaction data still yields reliable uncertainty for optimizing controllers.

Generative Models for Embodied Systems

Diffusion, flow matching, and related generative methods for planning and control — with structure and constraints so behaviors stay feasible and deployable on robots.

Dynamic Neural Koopman Distillation for Real-Time Robot Control Using Diffusion Models

Lei Zheng, Peiqi Yu, Zengqi Peng, Changliu Liu, and Armin Lederer · arXiv:2605.24924

Multistep diffusion planners are expressive but too slow for high-frequency closed-loop control. We distill the teacher into a one-step student via a Factorized Dynamic Koopman layer with state-dependent modal gains, retaining multimodal trajectory quality while cutting inference to the millisecond regime on MuJoCo and a Kinova Gen3.

SAD-Flower: Flow Matching for Safe, Admissible, and Dynamically Consistent Planning

T.-Y. Huang, Armin Lederer, D.-J. Wu, X. Dai, S. Zhang, S. Sosnowski, S.-H. Sun, and S. Hirche · ICML, 2026

Generative planners can propose diverse motions that violate dynamics or safety. SAD-Flower uses flow matching with constraints for safe, admissible, and dynamically consistent trajectories — bringing generative flexibility closer to what closed-loop robot controllers can actually execute.

Preprint Details

Toward Near-Globally Optimal Nonlinear Model Predictive Control via Diffusion Models

T.-Y. Huang, Armin Lederer, N. Hoischen, J. Brüdigam, X. Xiao, S. Sosnowski, and S. Hirche · L4DC, 2025

Nonconvex NMPC can get stuck in poor local optima. This work uses diffusion models to propose strong warm starts / candidates for nonlinear MPC, aiming at near-globally better closed-loop performance without abandoning the model-based optimal-control formulation.

Safe Learning Control

Safety filters, barrier certificates, and risk-sensitive mechanisms that wrap around learned policies — so autonomy can improve with data without leaving the safe set.

Gaussian Process-Based Real-Time Learning for Safety Critical Applications

Armin Lederer, A. Ordonez-Conejo, K. Maier, W. Xiao, J. Umlauft, and S. Hirche · ICML, 2021

Safety-critical control needs learning that keeps up with the plant. We develop real-time Gaussian-process learning methods that update models online with computational structure suited to closed-loop use, enabling data-driven adaptation without sacrificing the timing requirements of safety-critical applications.

Risk-Sensitive Safety Filters for Reinforcement Learning with Probabilistic Guarantees

Armin Lederer, E. Noorani, J. Baras, and S. Hirche · IEEE Transactions on Automatic Control, 2025

RL policies can be powerful yet unsafe. We design risk-sensitive safety filters with probabilistic guarantees that intervene when learned actions threaten safety, allowing aggressive learning while enforcing chance-constrained safety in closed loop.

PDF Preprint Details

Safe Learning-Based Control of Elastic Joint Robots via Control Barrier Functions

Armin Lederer, A. Begzadic, N. Das, and S. Hirche · IFAC, 2023

Elastic-joint robots are hard to model exactly, yet contacts and joint limits demand hard safety. This work combines learning-based dynamics with control barrier functions so that compliant manipulators can adapt from data while remaining within safe operating regions during experiments.
